What can/can't I bring in?

    YOU CAN BRING
  • Blankets
  • Two factory-sealed water bottles up to one liter each
  • Empty drinking container (e.g., Nalgene and Klean Kanteen water bottles)
  • Binoculars
  • Regular size backpacks, tote bags, purses, and one gallon zip top bags made of clear plastic, clear vinyl or clear PVC.
  • Small clutch bags and "Fanny-pack(s)" up to 6" x 8" x 3", approximately the size of your hand (DO NOT HAVE TO BE CLEAR)
  • Empty hydration packs with a gear capacity of 2.5L/150 cubic inches with no more than two pockets (DO NOT HAVE TO BE CLEAR)
    YOU CAN'T BRING
  • Any non-clear bag larger than the size of a fanny pack up to 6" x 8" x 3"
    *except for hydration packs with a gear capacity of 2.5L/150 cubic inches
  • Hydration packs with a gear capacity LARGER THAN 2.5L/150 cubic inches or with more than 2 pockets
  • Weapons of any kind
  • Illegal substances
  • Alcohol (Alcohol will be sold at the festival)
  • Glass containers of ANY kind
  • Cans of ANY kind
  • Video equipment (no video recording will be allowed)
  • Professional still camera equipment with a detachable lens longer than 2 inches, tripods, big zooms, or commercial use rigs
  • Audio recording equipment
  • Skateboards, scooters or personal motorized vehicles
  • Bicycles (Not allowed inside festival grounds)
  • Strollers
  • Fireworks and explosives
  • Instruments and laser pointers
How can I stay hydrated throughout the festival?

There are water refill stations located throughout the festival. Play your part by bringing a reusable container for free refills to stay hydrated all weekend.

Do vendors accept debit/credit cards?

All food, beer and merchandise stands will take credit card, ApplePay and cash payments.

Can I stay overnight in the park?

Dancing Through the Decades is not a camping festival and you cannot sleep in LA Historic park or your vehicle. No exceptions.

Do children get in for free?

Children under the age of 2 do not need a wristband to get into the festival.
